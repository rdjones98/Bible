Genesis 4

1 And the man knew Eve (Life) his wife; and she conceived and bore Cain (Acquired), and said, "I have acquired a man with the Lord (YHWH - He is)."

2 And she again bore his brother, Abel (Breath/Vanity). And Abel was a keeper of sheep, and Cain was a worker of the ground.

3 And it came to pass, at the end of days, that Cain brought of the fruit of the ground an offering to the Lord.

4 And Abel, he also brought of the firstborn of his flock and of their fat portions. And the Lord had regard for Abel and his offering,

5 But for Cain and for his offering He did not have regard. And Cain was very angry, and his face fell.

6 And the Lord said to Cain, "Why are you angry? And why has your face fallen?

7 If you do well, will there not be lifting up? But if you do not do well, sin is crouching at the door; and its desire is for you, but you must rule over it."

8 And Cain spoke to Abel his brother. And it came to pass, when they were in the field, that Cain rose up against Abel his brother and killed him.

9 And the Lord said to Cain, "Where is Abel your brother?" And he said, "I do not know. Am I my brother's keeper?"

10 And He said, "What have you done? The voice of your brother’s blood is crying out to Me from the ground.

11 And now you are cursed from the ground, which has opened its mouth to receive your brother’s blood from your hand.

12 When you work the ground, it shall no longer yield to you its strength. You shall be a fugitive and a wanderer on the earth."

13 And Cain said to the Lord, "My punishment is greater than I can bear.

14 Behold, You have driven me today away from the face of the ground, and from Your face I shall be hidden. And I shall be a fugitive and a wanderer on the earth, and whoever finds me will kill me."

15 And the Lord said to him, "Therefore, whoever kills Cain, vengeance shall be taken on him sevenfold." And the Lord set a mark on Cain, lest anyone finding him should strike him.

16 And Cain went out from the presence of the Lord, and settled in the land of Nod (Wandering), east of Eden (Delight).

17 And Cain knew his wife, and she conceived and bore Enoch (Dedicated). And he built a city and called the name of the city after the name of his son, Enoch (Dedicated).

18 And to Enoch was born Irad (Fugitive), and Irad fathered Mehujael (Smitten of God), and Mehujael fathered Methushael (Man of God), and Methushael fathered Lamech (Powerful).

19 And Lamech took two wives: the name of the one was Adah (Adornment), and the name of the second was Zillah (Shade).

20 And Adah bore Jabal (Stream/Move), he was the father of those who dwell in tents and have livestock.

21 And the name of his brother was Jubal (Sound/Stream), he was the father of all those who play the lyre and pipe.

22 And Zillah also bore Tubal-cain (Offspring of Smith), forger of every tool of bronze and iron. And the sister of Tubal-cain was Naamah (Pleasant).

23 And Lamech said to his wives, "Adah and Zillah, hear my voice; wives of Lamech, give ear to my speech! For I have killed a man for wounding me, a young man for striking me.

24 If Cain is avenged sevenfold, then Lamech seventy-sevenfold."

25 And Adam (Man) knew his wife again, and she bore a son and called his name Seth (Appointed), for she said, "God (Elohim) has appointed for me another offspring instead of Abel, because Cain killed him."

26 And to Seth also a son was born, and he called his name Enosh (Mortal/Man). Then began to call on the name of the Lord.
