Genesis 10

1This is the account of the families of the sons of Noah (Noach – Rest): Shem (Shem – Name), Ham (Ham – Hot), and Japheth (Yafeth – Fair); and the sons who were born to them after the flood.

2The sons of Japheth: Gomer (Gomer – Complete), Magog (Magog – Land of Gog), Madai (Madai – Middle), Javan (Yavan – Greece), Tubal (Tubal – To Be Changed), Meshech (Meshech – Drawing Out), and Tiras (Tiras – Desiring).

3The sons of Gomer: Ashkenaz (Ashkenaz – Germany), Riphath (Riphath – Ruin), and Togarmah (Togarmah – Barbarian).

4The sons of Javan: Elishah (Elishah – God Is My Salvation), Tarshish (Tarshish – A Stone), Kittim (Kittim – Cut-Off), and Dodanim (Dodanim – Beloved).

5From these the coastland peoples spread in their lands, each with its own language, by their clans and in their nations.

6The sons of Ham: Cush (Cush – Ethiopia), Mizraim (Mizraim – Egypt), Put (Put – Libyan), and Canaan (Kena'an – Lowland).

7The sons of Cush: Seba (Seba – Vail), Havilah (Havilah – Circle), Sabtah (Sabtah – Subtle), Raamah (Raamah – Thunder), and Sabtechah (Sabtechah – Subtle). The sons of Raamah: Sheba (Sheba – Oath) and Dedan (Dedan – Lowland).

8Cush was the father of Nimrod (Nimrod – Rebellious), who became a mighty warrior on the earth.

9He was a mighty hunter before the LORD (YHWH - He is); that is why it is said, “Like Nimrod, a mighty hunter before the LORD (YHWH - He is).”

10The first centers of his kingdom were Babylon (Bavel – Confusion), Erech (Urekh – City of the King), Akkad (Akkad – Mighty), and Calneh (Kalneh – Fortress), in Shinar (Shinar – Two Rivers).

11From that land he went to Assyria (Ashur – Ashur), where he built Nineveh (Ninveh – Fish), Rehoboth Ir (Reḥovot ʿIr – Streets of the City), Calah (Kalah – Complete),

12and Resen (Resen – Enclosure), which is between Nineveh and Calah. That is the great city.

13Mizraim was the father of the Ludim (Ludim – Lydians), Anamim (Anamim – Ancient), Lehabim (Lehabim – Flame), Naphtuhim (Naphtuhim – Broad),

14Pathrusim (Pathrusim – Southlanders), Casluhim (Kasluhim – Migrants) (from whom the Philistines (Pelishtim – Migrants) came) and Caphtorim (Kafṭorim – A Place of God).

15Canaan was the father of Sidon (Tsidon – Fisher) his firstborn, and of the Hittites (Hittim – Children of Heth),

16the Jebusites (Yevusim – People of Jerusalem), the Amorites (Emorim – Prominent), the Girgashites (Girgasim – Wanderers),

17the Hivites (Chivim – Villagers), the Arkites (Arkim – Arkan), the Sinites (Sinitim – The Sinites),

18the Arvadites (Arvadim – Arvad), the Zemarites (Zemarim – The Zemarites), and the Hamathites (Chamatim – Hamathites). Later the Canaanite clans scattered

19and the borders of Canaan reached from Sidon toward Gerar (Gerar – Rolling) as far as Gaza (Azah – Stronghold), and then toward Sodom (Sedom – Burning), Gomorrah (ʿAmorah – Submersion), Admah (Admah – Red), and Zeboyim (Tzevo’im – Station), as far as Lasha (Lasha – Captivity).

20These are the sons of Ham, by their clans and languages, in their territories and nations.

21Sons were also born to Shem, whose older brother was Japheth; Shem was the ancestor of all the sons of Eber (Eber – Beyond).

22The sons of Shem: Elam (Elam – Eternity), Ashur (Ashur – Ashur), Arphaxad (Arpakshad – Healer), Lud (Lud – Strife), and Aram (Aram – High).

23The sons of Aram: Uz (ʿUz – Strength), Hul (Ḥul – Spiral), Gether (Geter – Greatness), and Meshech (Meshech – Drawing Out).

24Arphaxad was the father of Shelah (Shelah – Petition), and Shelah the father of Eber.

25Two sons were born to Eber: One was named Peleg (Peleg – Division), because in his time the earth was divided; his brother was named Joktan (Yoqtan – Small).

26Joktan was the father of Almodad (Almodad – The Eternal), Sheleph (Shelaph – The Sent), Hazarmaveth (Ḥazarmavet – Court of Death), Jerah (Yerah – Moon),

27Hadoram (Hadoram – Glorious), Uzal (Uzal – Strong), Diklah (Diqlah – Palm Tree),

28Obal (Oval – Circle), Abimael (Avimael – Father of the King), Sheba (Sheba – Oath),

29Ophir (Ofir – Gold), Havilah (Havilah – Circle), and Jobab (Yovav – Persecutor).

30All these were the sons of Joktan.

31Their territory extended from Mesha toward Sephar (Sephar – Mount), the eastern hill country.

32These are the sons of Shem, by their clans and languages, in their territories and nations.

33These are the clans of the sons of Noah, according to their lines of descent, within their nations. From these the nations spread out over the earth after the flood.
