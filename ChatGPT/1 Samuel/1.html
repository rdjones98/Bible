1 Samuel 1

1There was a man from Ramathaim-zophim (Ramathayim-Tzofim – Two Heights of the Watchers), from the hill country of Ephraim (Efrayim – Fruitful), whose name was Elkanah (Elqanah – God has created), son of Jeroham (Yerocham – May he be shown mercy), son of Elihu (Elihu – He is my God), son of Tohu (Tochu – Sinking down), son of Zuph (Tzuph – Honeycomb), an Ephraimite.

2He had two wives: the name of one was Hannah (Channah – Grace), and the name of the other was Peninnah (Peninah – Pearl). Peninnah had children, but Hannah had none.

3This man went up from his city year after year to worship and to offer sacrifices to the LORD (YHWH – He is) of hosts at Shiloh (Shiloh – Tranquility), where the two sons of Eli (Eli – My God), Hophni (Chofni – Boxer) and Phinehas (Pinchas – Nubian or Mouth of Brass), were priests of the LORD.

4On the day Elkanah offered his sacrifice, he gave portions to his wife Peninnah and to all her sons and daughters.

5But to Hannah he gave a double portion, because he loved her, though the LORD had closed her womb.

6Her rival provoked her severely, to make her miserable, because the LORD had closed her womb.

7This happened year after year. Whenever she went up to the house of the LORD, Peninnah provoked her, and she would weep and not eat.

8Then Elkanah her husband said to her, “Hannah, why are you weeping? Why don’t you eat? Why is your heart sad? Am I not better to you than ten sons?”

9After they had eaten and drunk in Shiloh (Shiloh – Tranquility), Hannah rose. Now Eli the priest was sitting on the seat by the doorpost of the temple of the LORD.

10She was deeply distressed and prayed to the LORD, weeping bitterly.

11She made a vow, saying, “LORD of hosts (YHWH Tzeva'ot – He is commander of armies), if You will truly look on the affliction of Your servant and remember me, and not forget Your servant, but will give Your servant a male child, then I will give him to the LORD all the days of his life, and no razor shall touch his head.”

12As she continued praying before the LORD, Eli watched her mouth.

13Hannah was speaking in her heart; only her lips moved, but her voice was not heard. Therefore Eli thought she was drunk.

14And Eli said to her, “How long will you be drunk? Put away your wine!”

15But Hannah answered, “No, my lord, I am a woman of sorrowful spirit. I have not drunk wine or strong drink, but I have been pouring out my soul before the LORD.

16Do not regard your servant as a worthless woman. I have been speaking out of my great anxiety and grief.”

17Then Eli answered, “Go in peace, and may the God of Israel (Elohei Yisra’el) grant your request that you have asked of Him.”

18She said, “Let your servant find favor in your sight.” Then the woman went her way and ate, and her face was no longer sad.

19They rose early in the morning and worshiped before the LORD, then returned and came to their house at Ramah (Ramah – Height). And Elkanah knew Hannah his wife, and the LORD remembered her.

20In due time, Hannah conceived and bore a son, and she named him Samuel (Sh'mu'el – Heard by God), for she said, “I asked him from the LORD.”

21The man Elkanah went up with all his household to offer to the LORD the yearly sacrifice and his vow offering.

22But Hannah did not go up, for she said to her husband, “As soon as the boy is weaned, I will bring him, so that he may appear before the LORD and remain there forever.”

23Elkanah her husband said to her, “Do what seems best to you. Wait until you have weaned him; only may the LORD establish His word.” So the woman stayed and nursed her son until she weaned him.

24When she had weaned him, she took him up with her, along with a three-year-old bull, an ephah of flour, and a skin of wine. She brought him to the house of the LORD at Shiloh (Shiloh – Tranquility), and the child was young.

25They slaughtered the bull and brought the boy to Eli.

26She said, “Oh, my lord! As you live, my lord, I am the woman who stood here beside you, praying to the LORD.

27For this child I prayed, and the LORD has granted me my request that I made to Him.

28Therefore I have also dedicated him to the LORD; as long as he lives, he is given to the LORD.” And he worshiped the LORD there.
