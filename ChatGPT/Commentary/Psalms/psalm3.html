Psalm 3 and New Testament Fulfillment

Psalm 3 (Masoretic Text – Modern English) New Testament Reflection or Fulfillment
Verse 1
LORD (YHWH – He is), how many are my foes! Many are rising up against me.		 Luke 22:52–53 – Jesus is surrounded by enemies in the Garden of Gethsemane.
John 18:3–4 – A large crowd comes to arrest Him.
Verse 2
Many are saying of my soul, “There is no salvation for him in God (Elohim – Mighty Ones).”		 Matthew 27:43 – As Jesus hung on the cross, they mocked Him: “He trusts in God; let God deliver Him now.”
Fulfillment of enemies doubting that God would save Him.
Verse 3
But You, LORD (YHWH – He is), are a shield around me, my glory, and the One who lifts up my head.		 John 17:1,5 – Jesus prays that the Father would glorify Him.
Philippians 2:9–11 – God exalts Jesus and gives Him the name above all names.
Verse 4
I cried aloud to the LORD (YHWH – He is), and He answered me from His holy mountain. Selah.		 Hebrews 5:7 – Jesus offered prayers with loud cries and was heard because of His reverence.
His cry from Gethsemane reflects David’s desperation and trust.
Verse 5
I lay down and slept; I awoke, for the LORD (YHWH – He is) sustained me.		 Acts 2:24 – God raised Jesus from the dead, “freeing Him from the agony of death.”
David’s confidence in rising mirrors Jesus’ resurrection.
Verse 6
I will not be afraid of ten thousands of people who have set themselves against me all around.		 John 18:6 – When Jesus reveals Himself to the arresting mob, they fall back.
Matthew 26:53 – Jesus says He could call legions of angels, showing no fear.
Verse 7
Arise, LORD (YHWH – He is)! Save me, my God (Elohim – Mighty Ones)! For You strike all my enemies on the cheek; You break the teeth of the wicked.		 1 Corinthians 15:25 – Jesus must reign until He has put all enemies under His feet.
Revelation 19:15 – Jesus judges and strikes the nations with the sword from His mouth.
Verse 8
Salvation belongs to the LORD (YHWH – He is); Your blessing be on Your people. Selah.		 Acts 4:12 – “Salvation is found in no one else” but Jesus.
Revelation 7:10 – “Salvation belongs to our God… and to the Lamb.”
