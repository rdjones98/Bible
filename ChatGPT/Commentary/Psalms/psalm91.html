Psalm 91 and New Testament Fulfillment

Psalm 91 (Masoretic Text – Modern English) New Testament Reflection or Fulfillment
Verses 1–2
He who dwells in the secret place of the Most High (Elyon – Most High)... My God (Elohim – Mighty Ones), in whom I trust.		 John 14:10–11 – Jesus dwells in the Father and trusts fully in Him.
Luke 22:42 – “Not My will, but Yours be done” – Jesus' ultimate trust.
Verse 3
He will deliver you from the snare of the fowler and from deadly pestilence.		 Colossians 1:13 – Jesus delivers us from the domain of darkness.
John 11:4 – Jesus speaks of death not being final, even in the face of deadly illness.
Verses 4–6
He will cover you… You will not be afraid… pestilence… destruction...		 Matthew 8:26 – Jesus calms the storm, showing He is protector.
2 Timothy 1:7 – “God has not given us a spirit of fear...”
Verses 11–12
He will command His angels concerning you… they will lift you up...		 Matthew 4:5–7 – Satan quotes these verses to tempt Jesus.
Jesus refuses to misuse God’s promises and responds with Scripture, showing trust without presumption.
Verse 13
You will tread on the lion and the serpent...		 Luke 10:19 – Jesus gives His followers authority “to trample on serpents and scorpions.”
Romans 16:20 – “The God of peace will soon crush Satan under your feet.”
Verses 14–16
Because he has set his love on Me... I will deliver him... I will show him My salvation.		 Matthew 3:17 – “This is My beloved Son...”
Hebrews 5:7–9 – Jesus was heard because of His reverence and became the source of eternal salvation.
Philippians 2:9–11 – God exalts Jesus and gives Him the name above all names.
