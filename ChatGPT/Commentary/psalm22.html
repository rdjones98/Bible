Psalm 22 Verse Text (Modern English) Gospel Fulfillment
Verse 1 "My God, my God, why have You forsaken me?" Jesus cries this on the cross (Matthew 27:46; Mark 15:34)
Verses 6–7 "But I am a worm and not a man, scorned by mankind and despised by the people. All who see me mock me..." Jesus is mocked by passersby, priests, and soldiers (Matthew 27:39–44; Luke 23:35–36)
Verse 8 "He trusts in the LORD (YHWH – He is), let Him rescue him..." Identical taunt used by chief priests (Matthew 27:43)
Verse 14 "I am poured out like water, and all my bones are out of joint..." Suggests the physical trauma of crucifixion – extreme dehydration and dislocation
Verse 15 "My strength is dried up like a potsherd... You lay me in the dust of death." Jesus experiences extreme thirst (John 19:28) and death
Verse 16 "They pierced my hands and my feet" Roman crucifixion—nails in hands and feet (John 20:25–27)
(LXX and DSS support "pierced")
Verse 17 "I can count all my bones—they stare and gloat over me" Jesus is stripped and publicly shamed (John 19:23–24)
Verse 18 "They divide my garments among them and cast lots for my clothing" Roman soldiers divide His clothes and cast lots (John 19:23–24; Matthew 27:35)
Verse 22 "I will declare Your name to my brothers..." Quoted in Hebrews 2:12 as fulfilled by Jesus after His resurrection
Verses 27–31 "All the ends of the earth shall remember and turn to the LORD..." The global mission and worship of the risen Messiah (Matthew 28:19; Acts 1:8)
