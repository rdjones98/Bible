The Man with the Sword in Joshua 5

Great question — the identity of the man with the sword in Joshua 5:13–15 has been discussed and debated for centuries, and it’s a deeply rich moment in the text. Here’s a breakdown of what we can say from the text itself and how it's been interpreted:

📜 What the Text Says

In Joshua 5:13–15, Joshua meets a man with a drawn sword, who identifies himself as:

“Commander of the army of the Lord (YHWH – He is).”

Joshua falls to the ground in reverence, and the man tells him:

“Take your sandals off your feet, for the place where you are standing is holy.”

That echoes what was said to Moses at the burning bush (Exodus 3:5), where Moses encountered God Himself.

🔍 Interpretation #1: A Theophany (Appearance of YHWH Himself)

  • The language and setting (holy ground, worship not rebuked, direct command) strongly resemble divine encounters elsewhere in the Bible.
  • Some scholars and traditions say this is YHWH Himself, appearing in human-like form, also called a theophany.
  • Joshua bows down — and unlike other angelic encounters (like in Revelation), the figure does not tell him to stop. That suggests divinity.

🔍 Interpretation #2: The Angel (Messenger) of YHWH

  • Another very strong interpretation is that this is the Angel of the Lord — a special figure who speaks with God’s authority and at times seems indistinguishable from God (e.g., Genesis 16, Exodus 3, Judges 6).
  • In some traditions, the Angel of YHWH is considered a pre-incarnate appearance of the Messiah (a “Christophany”), especially in Christian theology.

⚔️ Summary:

Who is the man with the sword?

  • Most likely, he is either:
    • YHWH Himself appearing in human form (a theophany), or
    • The Angel of YHWH, who speaks as and for YHWH, often considered a visible, personal manifestation of God.

Either way, it’s no ordinary angel — this figure represents God’s personal leadership in the coming battle.
