Occurrences of "Tannin" (Sea Monster / Dragon / Serpent) in the Hebrew Bible

Verse Hebrew Form English Transliteration Translation Context & Meaning
Genesis 1:21 תַּנִּינִם tanninim great sea creatures God creates large marine life; possibly real animals (like whales) or symbolic creatures.
Exodus 7:9–10 תַּנִּין tannin serpent Aaron's staff becomes a serpent before Pharaoh; possibly larger or more mythic than a typical snake.
Exodus 7:12 תַּנִּינִם tanninim serpents The Egyptian magicians' staffs also become serpents; Aaron's swallows theirs.
Deuteronomy 32:33 תַּנִּינִם tanninim serpents Symbol of poisonous danger, referring to Israel’s enemies or their corruption.
Job 7:12 תַּנִּין tannin sea monster Job asks if he is a sea or a sea monster that must be guarded; metaphor for danger or chaos.
Psalm 74:13 תַּנִּינִים tanninim sea monsters God breaking the heads of sea monsters; likely mythic/chaotic forces.
Psalm 91:13 תַּנִּין tannin serpent or dragon Symbol of evil or danger; something the faithful will trample underfoot.
Psalm 148:7 תַּנִּינִים tanninim sea creatures / sea monsters Call for all creation, including sea monsters, to praise the LORD.
Isaiah 27:1 תַּנִּין tannin dragon Apocalyptic imagery of God slaying the dragon in the sea (chaos subdued).
Isaiah 51:9 תַּנִּין tannin dragon Refers to God’s past victory over chaos; poetic and mythic imagery.
Jeremiah 51:34 תַּנִּין tannin monster Babylon is metaphorically described as a monster that devours God’s people.
Ezekiel 29:3 תַּנִּין tannin dragon / crocodile Pharaoh is likened to a dragon or crocodile in the Nile; symbol of pride and coming judgment.
Ezekiel 32:2 תַּנִּין tannin dragon / sea monster Egypt is again symbolized as a sea monster stirring up the waters.
