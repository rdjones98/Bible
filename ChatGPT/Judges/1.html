Judges 1

1 After the death of Joshua (Yehoshua – YHWH is salvation), the children of Israel (Yisra’el – God strives) asked the LORD (YHWH – He is), saying, "Who shall go up first for us against the Canaanites (Kena‘anim – Lowlanders) to fight against them?"

2 The LORD said, "Judah (Yehudah – Praise) shall go up. Behold, I have given the land into his hand."

3 Judah said to Simeon (Shimon – Heard) his brother, "Come up with me into the territory allotted to me, that we may fight against the Canaanites, and I likewise will go with you into your territory." So Simeon went with him.

4 Then Judah went up, and the LORD gave the Canaanites and the Perizzites (Perizzi – Dwellers in open country) into their hand. They struck down ten thousand men at Bezek (Bezeq – Lightning).

5 They found Adoni-Bezek (Adoni-Bezeq – Lord of Lightning) at Bezek, fought against him, and struck the Canaanites and the Perizzites.

6 Adoni-Bezek fled, but they pursued him, seized him, and cut off his thumbs and big toes.

7 Adoni-Bezek said, "Seventy kings with their thumbs and big toes cut off used to gather scraps under my table. As I have done, so God has repaid me." They brought him to Jerusalem (Yerushalayim – Foundation of Peace), and he died there.

8 The children of Judah fought against Jerusalem, captured it, struck it with the edge of the sword, and set the city on fire.

9 Afterward, the children of Judah went down to fight against the Canaanites who lived in the hill country, the Negev (Negev – South), and the lowland.

10 Judah went against the Canaanites who lived in Hebron (Chevron – Association), and the name of Hebron was formerly Kiriath-Arba (Kiryat Arba – City of Four). They defeated Sheshai (Sheshai – Whitish), Ahiman (Achiman – My brother is a gift), and Talmai (Talmai – Furrowed).

11 From there they went against the inhabitants of Debir (Devir – Sanctuary), the name of Debir was formerly Kiriath-Sepher (Kiryat-Sefer – City of the Book).

12 Caleb (Kalev – Whole-hearted) said, "Whoever attacks Kiriath-Sepher and captures it, I will give him my daughter Achsah (Achsah – Anklet) as a wife."

13 Othniel (Otniel – God is my strength) the son of Kenaz (Kenaz – Hunter), Caleb's younger brother, captured it. So he gave him Achsah his daughter as a wife.

14 When she came to him, she urged him to ask her father for a field. She dismounted from her donkey, and Caleb said to her, "What do you want?"

15 She said to him, "Give me a blessing. Since you have given me land in the Negev (Negev – South), give me also springs of water." So Caleb gave her the upper springs and the lower springs.

16 The descendants of the Kenite (Qeini – Smith), Moses’ (Moshe – Drawn Out) father-in-law, went up from the City of Palms with the people of Judah into the wilderness of Judah, which lies in the Negev (Negev – South) near Arad (Arad – Fugitive), and they settled among the people.

17 Judah went with Simeon his brother, and they defeated the Canaanites living in Zephath (Tzefat – Watchtower), and utterly destroyed it. So the name of the city was called Hormah (Chormah – Destruction).

18 Judah also captured Gaza (Azzah – Strong), Ashkelon (Ashqelon – Weighing place), and Ekron (Eqron – Uprooted), with their territories.

19 The LORD was with Judah, and he took possession of the hill country. But he could not drive out the inhabitants of the plain, because they had chariots of iron.

20 They gave Hebron to Caleb, as Moses (Moshe) had said, and he drove out the three sons of Anak (Anak – Long-necked) from there.

21 But the children of Benjamin (Binyamin – Son of the right hand) did not drive out the Jebusites (Yevusi – Trampled), who lived in Jerusalem. So the Jebusites live with the children of Benjamin (Binyamin) in Jerusalem to this day.

22 The house of Joseph (Yosef – He adds) also went up against Bethel (Beit-El – House of God), and the LORD was with them.

23 The house of Joseph sent spies to Bethel (Beit-El) (the name of the city before was Luz – Almond Tree).

24 The spies saw a man coming out of the city and said to him, "Please show us the entrance to the city, and we will deal kindly with you."

25 He showed them the entrance to the city, and they struck the city with the edge of the sword, but they let the man and all his family go free.

26 The man went to the land of the Hittites (Chitti – Terror), built a city, and called its name Luz (Luz – Almond Tree), which is its name to this day.

27 Manasseh (Menasheh – Causing to forget) did not drive out the inhabitants of Beth Shean (Beit-Shean – House of Rest) and its villages, or Taanach (Ta’anakh – Sandy) and its villages, or the inhabitants of Dor (Dor – Dwelling) and its villages, or the inhabitants of Ibleam (Yivle‘am – Devours the people) and its villages, or the inhabitants of Megiddo (Megiddo – Place of Troops) and its villages; so the Canaanites persisted in living in that land.

28 When Israel grew strong, they put the Canaanites to forced labor, but did not completely drive them out.

29 Ephraim (Efrayim – Fruitful) did not drive out the Canaanites who lived in Gezer (Gezer – Portion), so the Canaanites lived among them in Gezer.

30 Zebulun (Zevulun – Dwelling) did not drive out the inhabitants of Kitron (Kitron – Incense) or Nahalol (Nachalol – Valley), so the Canaanites lived among them and became forced laborers.

31 Asher (Asher – Happy) did not drive out the inhabitants of Acco (Akko – Hot Sand), or Sidon (Tzidon – Fishery), or Ahlab (Achlav – Fatness), or Achzib (Achziv – Deceit), or Helbah (Chelbah – Fertility), or Aphik (Afik – Fortress), or Rehob (Rechov – Broad Place).

32 So the Asherites lived among the Canaanites, the inhabitants of the land, for they did not drive them out.

33 Naphtali (Naftali – My struggle) did not drive out the inhabitants of Beth Shemesh (Beit-Shemesh – House of the Sun) or Beth Anath (Beit-Anat – House of Affliction), but lived among the Canaanites, the inhabitants of the land. The inhabitants of Beth Shemesh and Beth Anath became forced labor for them.

34 The Amorites (Emori – Talkers) forced the children of Dan (Dan – Judge) into the hill country, for they would not allow them to come down into the valley.

35 The Amorites persisted in living in Mount Heres (Cheres – Sun), in Aijalon (Ayalon – Place of Deer), and in Shaalbim (Shaalvim – Foxes), but the hand of the house of Joseph prevailed, so they became forced labor.

36 The border of the Amorites extended from the Ascent of Akrabbim (Ma‘aleh-Akrabbim – Ascent of Scorpions), from Sela (Sela – Rock), and upward.
