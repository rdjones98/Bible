Exodus 13 - Straightforward Translation

1. The Lord (YHWH - Lord) spoke (Dabar - spoke) to Moses (Mosheh - drawn out), saying (Amar - saying),

2. "Consecrate (Qadash - consecrate) to me all the firstborn (Bekhor - firstborn), whatever opens (Patach - opens) the womb (Rechem - womb) among the people of Israel (Yisra'el - Israel), both of man (Ish - man) and of beast (Behemah - beast); it is mine (Li - mine)."

3. And Moses (Mosheh - drawn out) said to the people, "Remember (Zakar - remember) this day (Yom - day) in which you came out (Yatsa - came out) from Egypt (Mitzrayim - Egypt), out of the house of bondage (Abodah - bondage), for by strength (Chazakah - strength) of hand (Yad - hand) the Lord (YHWH - Lord) brought you out (Yatsa - brought you out) from this place (Makam - place). No leavened bread (Chameitz - leavened bread) shall be eaten (Achal - eaten).

4. On this day (Yom - day) you are going out (Yatsa - going out) in the month (Chodesh - month) of Abib (Aviv - Abib).

5. And it shall be when the Lord (YHWH - Lord) brings you into the land (Eretz - land) of the Canaanites (Kena'an - Canaanites), the Hittites (Chittim - Hittites), the Amorites (Emorim - Amorites), the Perizzites (Perizzim - Perizzites), the Hivites (Chivim - Hivites), and the Jebusites (Yevusim - Jebusites), which he swore (Nishba - swore) to your fathers (Avot - fathers), to give (Natan - give) you a land (Eretz - land) flowing (Zav - flowing) with milk (Chalav - milk) and honey (Dvash - honey), that you shall keep (Shamar - keep) this service (Avodah - service) in this month (Chodesh - month).

6. Seven (Sheba - seven) days (Yamim - days) you shall eat (Achal - eat) unleavened bread (Matzah - unleavened bread), and on the seventh (Shvi'i - seventh) day (Yom - day) there shall be a feast (Chag - feast) to the Lord (YHWH - Lord).

7. Unleavened bread (Matzah - unleavened bread) shall be eaten (Achal - eaten) throughout the seven (Sheba - seven) days (Yamim - days), and no leavened bread (Chameitz - leavened bread) shall be seen (Ra'ah - seen) among you, and no leaven (Se'or - leaven) shall be seen (Ra'ah - seen) among you in all your territory (Gevul - territory).

8. You shall tell (Yadah - tell) your son (Ben - son) on that day (Yom - day), saying (Amar - saying), 'It is because of what the Lord (YHWH - Lord) did for me when I came out (Yatsa - came out) of Egypt (Mitzrayim - Egypt).'

9. It shall be as a sign (Oth - sign) to you on your hand (Yad - hand), and as a memorial (Zikaron - memorial) between your eyes (Ein - eyes), that the law (Torah - law) of the Lord (YHWH - Lord) may be in your mouth (Peh - mouth); for with a strong (Chazakah - strong) hand (Yad - hand) the Lord (YHWH - Lord) brought you out (Yatsa - brought you out) of Egypt (Mitzrayim - Egypt).

10. You shall therefore keep (Shamar - keep) this ordinance (Chuqah - ordinance) at its appointed time (Mo'ed - appointed time) from year (Shanah - year) to year (Shanah - year).

11. "And it shall be when the Lord (YHWH - Lord) brings you into the land (Eretz - land) of the Canaanites (Kena'an - Canaanites), as he swore (Nishba - swore) to you and to your fathers (Avot - fathers), and gives (Natan - gives) it to you,

12. that you shall set apart (Hefresh - set apart) to the Lord (YHWH - Lord) all that open (Patach - open) the womb (Rechem - womb), that is, every firstborn (Bekhor - firstborn) of your livestock (Miqneh - livestock); the males (Zachar - males) shall be the Lord’s (YHWH - Lord’s).

13. But every firstborn (Bekhor - firstborn) of a donkey (Hamor - donkey) you shall redeem (Padah - redeem) with a lamb (Kebes - lamb); and if you will not redeem (Padah - redeem) it, you shall break (Shabar - break) its neck (Horef - neck). And all the firstborn (Bekhor - firstborn) of man (Ish - man) among your sons (Banim - sons) you shall redeem (Padah - redeem).

14. And when in time to come (Bo - come), your son (Ben - son) asks you, 'What does this mean?' you shall say (Amar - say) to him, 'By strength (Chazakah - strength) of hand (Yad - hand) the Lord (YHWH - Lord) brought us out (Yatsa - brought us out) from Egypt (Mitzrayim - Egypt), from the house of bondage (Abodah - bondage).

15. And when Pharaoh (Par'oh - Pharaoh) stubbornly (Chazak - stubbornly) refused (Ma'an - refused) to let us go (Shalach - let us go), the Lord (YHWH - Lord) killed (Haraq - killed) all the firstborn (Bekhor - firstborn) in the land (Eretz - land) of Egypt (Mitzrayim - Egypt), both the firstborn (Bekhor - firstborn) of man (Ish - man) and the firstborn (Bekhor - firstborn) of beast (Behemah - beast). Therefore I sacrifice (Zabach - sacrifice) to the Lord (YHWH - Lord) all the males (Zachar - males) that first open (Patach - open) the womb (Rechem - womb), but all the firstborn (Bekhor - firstborn) of my sons (Banim - sons) I redeem (Padah - redeem).'

16. It shall be as a mark (Oth - mark) on your hand (Yad - hand) and as frontlets (Tefillin - frontlets) between your eyes (Ein - eyes), for by strength (Chazakah - strength) of hand (Yad - hand) the Lord (YHWH - Lord) brought us out (Yatsa - brought us out) of Egypt (Mitzrayim - Egypt)."

17. When Pharaoh (Par'oh - Pharaoh) let the people go, God (Elohim - God) did not lead (Nahal - lead) them by the way (Derekh - way) of the land (Eretz - land) of the Philistines (Pelishtim - Philistines), although that was near (Karov - near), for God (Elohim - God) said, "Lest the people change their minds (Yashav - change their minds) when they see (Ra'ah - see) war (Milhamah - war) and return (Shub - return) to Egypt (Mitzrayim - Egypt)."



18. But God (Elohim - God) led (Nahal - led) the people around (Sabh - around) by the way (Derekh - way) of the wilderness (Midbar - wilderness) toward the Red Sea (Yam Suf - Red Sea). And the people of Israel (Yisra'el - Israel) went up (Alah - went up) out of the land (Eretz - land) of Egypt (Mitzrayim - Egypt) equipped (Chazakah - equipped) for battle (Milhamah - battle).

19. And Moses (Mosheh - drawn out) took (Lakah - took) the bones (Atzem - bones) of Joseph (Yosef - Joseph) with him, for Joseph (Yosef - Joseph) had made (Shaba - made) the children (Banim - children) of Israel (Yisra'el - Israel) solemnly swear (Shaba - swear), saying (Amar - saying), "God (Elohim - God) will surely (Pakod - surely) visit (Paqad - visit) you, and you shall carry up (Ala - carry up) my bones (Atzem - bones) with you from here (Mikan - from here)."

20. And they moved (Nasa - moved) on from Succoth (Sukkot - Succoth) and encamped (Chanah - encamped) at Etham (Eitam - Etham), on the edge (Katsah - edge) of the wilderness (Midbar - wilderness).

21. And the Lord (YHWH - Lord) went before them by day (Yom - day) in a pillar (Amud - pillar) of cloud (Anan - cloud), to lead them along the way (Derekh - way), and by night (Lailah - night) in a pillar (Amud - pillar) of fire (Esh - fire), to give them light (Or - light), that they might travel (Halak - travel) by day (Yom - day) and by night (Lailah - night).

22. The pillar (Amud - pillar) of cloud (Anan - cloud) by day (Yom - day) and the pillar (Amud - pillar) of fire (Esh - fire) by night (Lailah - night) did not depart (Azav - depart) from before (Mipney - before) the people.
