Exodus 2

1. Now a man of the house of Levi (Levi - joined) went and took as his wife a daughter (Bat - daughter) of Levi (Levi - joined).

2. The woman conceived (Harah - conceived) and gave birth to a son (Ben - son). When she saw that he was a fine child (Tov - good), she hid him for three months (Shalosh - three months).

3. But when she could hide him no longer, she took for him a basket of bulrushes (Gomeh - papyrus), and coated it with tar (Chomer - pitch) and with pitch (Zefet - pitch). She placed the child in it and laid it among the reeds (Suf - reeds) by the bank (Saf - edge) of the Nile.

4. His sister stood at a distance, to see what would be done to him.

5. The daughter (Bat - daughter) of Pharaoh came down to bathe at the river, and her maidens (Shifchah - maidens) walked along the riverbank. She saw the basket among the reeds (Suf - reeds), and sent her servant (Amat - maidservant) to get it.

6. When she opened it, she saw the child, and behold, the baby was crying. She had pity (Racham - compassion) on him and said, "This is one of the Hebrews' children (Yeled - children)."

7. Then his sister said to Pharaoh's daughter (Bat - daughter), "Shall I go and call a nurse (Me'eneket - nurse) for you from the Hebrew women, that she may nurse the child for you?"

8. Pharaoh's daughter (Bat - daughter) said to her, "Go." So the girl went and called the child's mother (Em - mother).

9. Pharaoh's daughter (Bat - daughter) said to her, "Take this child away and nurse him for me, and I will pay you (Shalam - pay). So the woman took the child and nursed (Yenik - nursed) him.

10. The child grew (Gadal - grew), and she brought him to Pharaoh's daughter (Bat - daughter), and he became her son (Ben - son). She named him Moses (Mosheh - drawn out), saying, "Because I drew him out (Mashah - drew out) of the water."

11. In those days, when Moses (Mosheh - drawn out) had grown up, he went out to his people and looked on their hard labor (Sivlot - burdens). He saw an Egyptian (Mitzri - Egyptian) beating (Nakah - striking) a Hebrew, one of his people.

12. He looked around and saw that there was no one around, so he struck the Egyptian (Mitzri - Egyptian) and hid (Nasan - hid) him in the sand (Chol - sand).

13. When he went out the next day, behold, two Hebrews were fighting. He said to the one who did the wrong (Rasha - wrong), "Why are you striking (Nakah - striking) your companion (Re'a - fellow)?"

14. The man said, "Who made you a prince (Sar - ruler) and a judge over us? Do you mean to kill me as you killed the Egyptian (Mitzri - Egyptian)?" Then Moses (Mosheh - drawn out) was afraid (Yare - afraid), and thought (Chashav - thought), "Surely the matter (Dabar - matter) is known (Yadah - known)."

15. When Pharaoh heard of this matter (Dabar - matter), he sought (Baqash - sought) to kill Moses (Mosheh - drawn out). But Moses (Mosheh - drawn out) fled (Nadad - fled) from Pharaoh and settled (Yashav - dwelt) in the land of Midian (Midyan - strife), and sat down (Yashav - sat) by a well (Be'er - well).

16. Now the priest (Kohen - priest) of Midian (Midyan - strife) had seven daughters (Bat - daughters), and they came to draw water (Dahm - draw water) and filled the troughs (Ravah - troughs) to water (Shatah - water) their father's flock (Tzon - flock).

17. The shepherds (Roeh - shepherds) came and drove them away (Ra'ash - drove away), but Moses (Mosheh - drawn out) stood up (Amad - stood up) and helped (Yasha - helped) them and watered (Shatah - watered) their flock (Tzon - flock).

18. When they came to Reuel (R'uel - friend of God), their father (Av - father), he said, "Why have you come back (Bo - returned) so soon (Mahar - soon) today?"

19. They said, "An Egyptian (Mitzri - Egyptian) delivered (Matsal - delivered) us from the hand of the shepherds (Roeh - shepherds), and also drew water (Dahm - drew water) for us and watered (Shatah - watered) the flock (Tzon - flock)."

20. He said to his daughters (Bat - daughters), "Where is he? Why have you left (Azab - left) the man? Call (Qara - call) him, that he may eat (Akal - eat) bread (Lechem - bread)."

21. Moses (Mosheh - drawn out) agreed to stay) with the man, and he gave Moses (Mosheh - drawn out) his daughter (Bat - daughter) Zipporah (Tzipporah - bird) as his wife.

22. She gave birth to a son (Ben - son), and he his name Gershom (Gershom - a stranger there), for he said, "I have been a stranger (Ger - stranger) in a foreign land."

23. After a long time (Yamim - days) the king of Egypt died, and the Israelites (Bnei - sons of Israel) groaned because of their slavery and cried out (Tza'ak - cried out). Their cry (Tza'ak - cry) for help (Shav - help) because of their slavery went up to God (Elohim - God).

24. God (Elohim - God) heard their groaning and remembered his covenant with Abraham (Avraham - father of many), with Isaac (Yitzhak - laughter), and with Jacob (Yaakov - supplanted).

25. God (Elohim - God) saw the Israelites (Bnei - sons of Israel) and knew about their suffering.
