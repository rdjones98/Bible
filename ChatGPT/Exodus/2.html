Exodus 2 - Straightforward Translation

1. Now a man (Ish - man) of the house (Bait - house) of Levi (Levi - joined) went and took (Lakah - took) as his wife (Isha - wife) a daughter (Bat - daughter) of Levi (Levi - joined).

2. The woman (Isha - woman) conceived (Harah - conceived) and gave birth (Yalad - gave birth) to a son (Ben - son). When she saw that he was a fine child (Tov - good), she hid him for three months (Shalosh - three months).

3. But when she could hide him no longer, she took for him a basket (Tevat - basket) of bulrushes (Gomeh - papyrus), and coated it with tar (Chomer - pitch) and with pitch (Zefet - pitch). She placed the child (Yeled - child) in it and laid it among the reeds (Suf - reeds) by the bank (Saf - edge) of the Nile (Yor - river).

4. His sister (Achot - sister) stood at a distance (Rechok - distance), to see what would be done (Asah - done) to him.

5. The daughter (Bat - daughter) of Pharaoh (Par'oh - Pharaoh) came down (Yarad - came down) to bathe (Rachats - bathe) at the river (Yor - river), and her maidens (Shifchah - maidens) walked along the riverbank (Yad - hand). She saw the basket (Tevat - basket) among the reeds (Suf - reeds), and sent her servant (Amat - maidservant) to get it.

6. When she opened it, she saw the child (Yeled - child), and behold, the baby (Taph - infant) was crying (Bachah - crying). She had pity (Racham - compassion) on him and said, "This is one of the Hebrews' (Ivrim - Hebrews) children (Yeled - children)."

7. Then his sister (Achot - sister) said to Pharaoh's (Par'oh - Pharaoh's) daughter (Bat - daughter), "Shall I go and call a nurse (Me'eneket - nurse) for you from the Hebrew (Ivrim - Hebrews) women (Nashim - women), that she may nurse (Yenik - nurse) the child (Yeled - child) for you?"

8. Pharaoh's (Par'oh - Pharaoh's) daughter (Bat - daughter) said to her, "Go." So the girl (Na'arah - girl) went and called the child's (Yeled - child) mother (Em - mother).

9. Pharaoh's (Par'oh - Pharaoh's) daughter (Bat - daughter) said to her, "Take this child (Yeled - child) away and nurse (Yenik - nurse) him for me, and I will pay you (Shalam - pay). So the woman (Isha - woman) took the child (Yeled - child) and nursed (Yenik - nursed) him.

10. The child (Yeled - child) grew (Gadal - grew), and she brought him to Pharaoh's (Par'oh - Pharaoh's) daughter (Bat - daughter), and he became her son (Ben - son). She named him Moses (Mosheh - drawn out), saying, "Because I drew him out (Mashah - drew out) of the water (Mayim - water)."

11. In those days, when Moses (Mosheh - drawn out) had grown up (Gadal - grown), he went out (Yatza - went out) to his people (Am - people) and looked (Ra'ah - saw) on their hard labor (Sivlot - burdens). He saw an Egyptian (Mitzri - Egyptian) beating (Nakah - striking) a Hebrew (Ivri - Hebrew), one of his people (Am - people).

12. He looked around (Savar - looked) and saw that there was no one (Ish - man) around, so he struck (Nakah - struck) the Egyptian (Mitzri - Egyptian) and hid (Nasan - hid) him in the sand (Chol - sand).

13. When he went out (Yatza - went out) the next day, behold, two Hebrews (Ivrim - Hebrews) were fighting (Natsah - fighting). He said to the one who did the wrong (Rasha - wrong), "Why are you striking (Nakah - striking) your companion (Re'a - fellow)?"

14. The man (Ish - man) said, "Who made you a prince (Sar - ruler) and a judge (Shaphat - judge) over us? Do you mean to kill (Harag - kill) me as you killed (Harag - killed) the Egyptian (Mitzri - Egyptian)?" Then Moses (Mosheh - drawn out) was afraid (Yare - afraid), and thought (Chashav - thought), "Surely the matter (Dabar - matter) is known (Yadah - known)."

15. When Pharaoh (Par'oh - Pharaoh) heard (Shama - heard) of this matter (Dabar - matter), he sought (Baqash - sought) to kill (Harag - kill) Moses (Mosheh - drawn out). But Moses (Mosheh - drawn out) fled (Nadad - fled) from Pharaoh (Par'oh - Pharaoh) and settled (Yashav - dwelt) in the land (Eretz - land) of Midian (Midyan - strife), and sat down (Yashav - sat) by a well (Be'er - well).

16. Now the priest (Kohen - priest) of Midian (Midyan - strife) had seven daughters (Bat - daughters), and they came (Bo - came) to draw water (Dahm - draw water) and filled the troughs (Ravah - troughs) to water (Shatah - water) their father's flock (Tzon - flock).

17. The shepherds (Roeh - shepherds) came and drove them away (Ra'ash - drove away), but Moses (Mosheh - drawn out) stood up (Amad - stood up) and helped (Yasha - helped) them and watered (Shatah - watered) their flock (Tzon - flock).

18. When they came (Bo - came) to Reuel (R'uel - friend of God), their father (Av - father), he said, "Why have you come back (Bo - returned) so soon (Mahar - soon) today?"

19. They said, "An Egyptian (Mitzri - Egyptian) delivered (Matsal - delivered) us from the hand (Yad - hand) of the shepherds (Roeh - shepherds), and also drew water (Dahm - drew water) for us and watered (Shatah - watered) the flock (Tzon - flock)."

20. He said to his daughters (Bat - daughters), "Where is he? Why have you left (Azab - left) the man (Ish - man)? Call (Qara - call) him, that he may eat (Akal - eat) bread (Lechem - bread)."

21. Moses (Mosheh - drawn out) agreed (Yashav - agreed) to stay (Yashav - stay) with the man (Ish - man), and he gave (Natan - gave) Moses (Mosheh - drawn out) his daughter (Bat - daughter) Zipporah (Tzipporah - bird) as his wife (Isha - wife).

22. She gave birth (Yalad - gave birth) to a son (Ben - son), and he called (Qara - called) his name (Shem - name) Gershom (Gershom - a stranger there), for he said, "I have been a stranger (Ger - stranger) in a foreign land (Eretz - land)."

23. After a long time (Yamim - days) the king (Melech - king) of Egypt (Mitzrayim - Egypt) died (Muth - died), and the Israelites (Bnei - sons of Israel) groaned (Sha'ah - groaned) because of their slavery (Avodah - slavery) and cried out (Tza'ak - cried out). Their cry (Tza'ak - cry) for help (Shav - help) because of their slavery (Avodah - slavery) went up to God (Elohim - God).

24. God (Elohim - God) heard (Shama - heard) their groaning (Na'akah - groaning) and remembered (Zakar - remembered) his covenant (Brit - covenant) with Abraham (Avraham - father of many), with Isaac (Yitzhak - laughter), and with Jacob (Yaakov - supplanted).

25. God (Elohim - God) saw (Ra'ah - saw) the Israelites (Bnei - sons of Israel) and knew (Yadah - knew) about their suffering (Oneh - suffering).
