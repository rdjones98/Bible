Exodus 31

The Appointment of Bezalel and Oholiab

1 The LORD (YHWH) spoke to Moses (Moses, meaning "drawn out" or "saved from the water"), saying,

2 "See, I have called by name Bezalel (Bezalel, meaning "in the shadow of God"), the son of Uri (Uri, meaning "my light"), the son of Hur (Hur, meaning "free" or "white"), of the tribe of Judah (Yehudah, meaning "praise").

3 I have filled him with the Spirit of God (Ruach Elohim), in wisdom, in understanding, and in knowledge, and in all manner of workmanship,

4 to devise cunning works, to work in gold, and in silver, and in brass,

5 and in cutting of stones, to set them, and in carving of timber, to work in all manner of workmanship.

6 And I, behold, I have given with him Oholiab (Oholiab, meaning "father’s tent"), the son of Ahisamach (Ahisamach, meaning "brother of support"), of the tribe of Dan (Dan, meaning "judgment"); and in the hearts of all that are wise-hearted I have put wisdom, that they may make all that I have commanded you.

The Tabernacle

7 The tabernacle of the congregation, and the ark of the testimony, and the mercy seat (kaporet) that is upon it, and all the furniture of the tabernacle,

8 and the table and his vessels, and the pure lampstand with all its vessels, and the altar of incense,

9 and the altar of burnt offering with all his vessels, and the laver and his foot,

10 and the finely worked garments, and the holy garments for Aaron the priest, and the garments of his sons, to minister in the priest’s office,

11 and the anointing oil (shemen hamishchah), and sweet incense for the holy place: according to all that I have commanded you shall they do.

The Sabbath

12 And the LORD (YHWH) spoke to Moses, saying,

13 "Speak you also to the children of Israel (Yisrael, meaning "he who strives with God"), saying, 'My sabbaths you shall keep: for it is a sign between me and you throughout your generations; that you may know that I am the LORD (YHWH) that does sanctify you.'

14 You shall keep the sabbath therefore; for it is holy to you: every one that defiles it shall surely be put to death: for whosoever does any work therein, that soul shall be cut off from among his people.

15 Six days may work be done; but in the seventh is the sabbath of rest, holy to the LORD (YHWH): whosoever does any work in the sabbath day, he shall surely be put to death.

16 The children of Israel (Yisrael) shall keep the sabbath, to observe the sabbath throughout their generations, for a perpetual covenant.

17 It is a sign between me and the children of Israel (Yisrael) forever: for in six days the LORD (YHWH) made heaven and earth, and on the seventh day he rested, and was refreshed."

Moses Receives the Tablets of the Law

18 And he gave to Moses, when he had made an end of communing with him upon mount Sinai (Sinai, meaning "thorn" or "bush"), two tables of testimony, tables of stone, written with the finger of God (YHWH).
