Exodus 21 - Literal Translation

1. "Now these are the rules that you shall set before them:

2. When you buy a Hebrew slave (ebed - "servant"), he shall serve six years. And in the seventh he shall go out free, for nothing.

3. If he comes in single, he shall go out single. If he comes in married, then his wife shall go out with him.

4. If his master gives him a wife, and she bears him sons or daughters, the wife and her children shall be her master's, and he shall go out alone.

5. But if the slave plainly says, 'I love my master (adonai - "my lord"), my wife (ishah - "woman") and my children (ben - "son," bat - "daughter"), I will not go out free,'

6. then his master shall bring him to God (Elohim - "God"), and he shall bring him to the door or the doorpost. And his master shall bore his ear through with an awl, and he shall be his slave (ebed - "servant") forever.

7. When a man (ish - "man") sells his daughter (bat - "daughter") as a slave (amah - "female servant"), she shall not go out as the male slaves (ebed - "servants") do.

8. If she does not please her master (adonai - "my lord"), who has designated her for himself, then he shall let her be redeemed. He shall have no right to sell her to a foreign people, since he has broken faith with her.

9. If he designates her for his son (ben - "son"), he shall deal with her as with a daughter (bat - "daughter").

10. If he takes another wife (ishah - "woman"), he shall not diminish her food, her clothing, or her marital rights.

11. And if he does not do these three things for her, she shall go out for nothing, without payment of money.

12. Whoever strikes a man (ish - "man") so that he dies shall be put to death.

13. But if he did not lie in wait for him, but God (Elohim - "God") let him fall into his hand, then I will appoint for you a place to which he may flee.

14. But if a man (ish - "man") willfully attacks another to kill him by cunning, you shall take him from my altar, that he may die.

15. Whoever kidnaps a man (ish - "man") and sells him, and anyone found in possession of him, shall be put to death.

16. Whoever strikes his father (av - "father") or his mother (imah - "mother") shall be put to death.

17. Whoever steals a man (ish - "man") and sells him, and anyone found in possession of him, shall be put to death.

18. When men (ish - "men") quarrel and one strikes the other with a stone or with his fist, and the man (ish - "man") does not die but takes to his bed,

19. then if the man (ish - "man") rises again and walks outdoors with his staff, he who struck him shall be clear; only he shall pay for his loss of time, and shall have him thoroughly healed.

20. When a man (ish - "man") strikes his slave (ebed - "servant") or his female slave (amah - "female servant"), with a rod, and the slave dies under his hand, he shall be avenged.

21. But if the slave survives a day or two, he is not to be avenged, for the slave is his money.

22. When men (ish - "men") strive together and hit a pregnant woman (ishah - "woman") so that her children come out, but there is no harm, the one who hit her shall surely be fined, as the woman's husband (ish - "man") shall impose on him, and he shall pay as the judges determine.

23. But if there is harm, then you shall pay life for life,

24. eye for eye, tooth for tooth, hand for hand, foot for foot,

25. burn for burn, wound for wound, stripe for stripe.

26. When a man (ish - "man") strikes the eye of his slave (ebed - "servant") or the eye of his female slave (amah - "female servant"), and destroys it, he shall let him go free because of his eye.

27. If he knocks out the tooth of his slave (ebed - "servant") or the tooth of his female slave (amah - "female servant"), he shall let him go free because of his tooth.

28. When an ox (shor - "ox") gores a man (ish - "man") or a woman (ishah - "woman") to death, the ox (shor - "ox") shall surely be stoned, and its flesh shall not be eaten. But the owner of the ox (shor - "ox") shall be clear.

29. But if the ox (shor - "ox") has been accustomed to gore in the past, and its owner has been warned but has not kept it in, and it kills a man (ish - "man") or a woman (ishah - "woman"), the ox (shor - "ox") shall be stoned, and its owner also shall be put to death.

30. If a ransom is imposed on him, then he shall give for the redemption of his life whatever is imposed on him.

31. If it gores a son (ben - "son") or a daughter (bat - "daughter"), it shall be done to him according to this rule.

32. If the ox (shor - "ox") gores a male or female servant (ebed - "servant," amah - "female servant"), the owner shall give to their master thirty shekels of silver, and the ox (shor - "ox") shall be stoned.

33. When a man (ish - "man") opens a pit, or when a man (ish - "man") digs a pit and does not cover it, and an ox (shor - "ox") or a donkey (chamor - "donkey") falls into it,

34. the owner of the pit shall make restitution. He shall give money to its owner, and the dead beast (chayah - "animal") shall be his.

35. When one man's (ish - "man") ox (shor - "ox") hurts another's ox (shor - "ox"), so that it dies, then they shall sell the live ox (shor - "ox") and divide its price. And the dead ox (shor - "ox") they shall also divide.

36. Or if it is known that the ox (shor - "ox") has been accustomed to gore in the past, and its owner has not kept it in, he shall pay ox (shor - "ox") for ox (shor - "ox"), and the dead beast (chayah - "animal") shall be his.
