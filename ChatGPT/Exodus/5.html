Exodus 5

1. Afterward Moses (Mosheh - drawn out) and Aaron (Aharon - exalted) went and said to Pharaoh, "Thus says the Lord (YHWH - Lord), the God (Elohim - God) of Israel (Yisrael - God contends), 'Let my people go that they may hold (Chagag - keep) a feast (Chag - feast) to me in the wilderness (Midbar - wilderness).'"

2. But Pharaoh said, "Who is the Lord (YHWH - Lord), that I should obe his voice and let Israel (Yisrael - God contends) go? I do not know the Lord (YHWH - Lord), and moreover, I will not let Israel (Yisrael - God contends) go."

3. Then they said, "The God (Elohim - God) of the Hebrews has met with us. Please let us go a three-day journey (Derekh - journey) into the wilderness (Midbar - wilderness) that we may sacrifice (Zabach - sacrifice) to the Lord (YHWH - Lord) our God (Elohim - God), lest he fall upon us with pestilence (Dever - pestilence) or with the sword (Cherev - sword)."

4. But the king of Egypt said to them, "Moses (Mosheh - drawn out) and Aaron (Aharon - exalted), why do you take the people away from their work? Get back to your burdens."

5. And Pharaoh said, "Behold, the people of the land are now many, and you make them rest (Shabath - rest) from their burdens."

6. The same day Pharaoh commanded the taskmasters (Sareh - officers) of the people and their foremen (Shoter - overseers), "You shall no longer give the people straw to make bricks, as in the past (Re'shon - former days); let them go and gather straw for themselves.

7. But the number of bricks that they made in the past (Re'shon - former days) you shall impose on them; you shall by no means reduce it, for they are idle; therefore they cry (Zaaq - cry), 'Let us go and offer (Zabach - offer) sacrifice (Zabach - sacrifice) to our God (Elohim - God).'

8. Let heavier (Kaved - heavy) work be laid on the men, that they may labor (Avad - work) at it and pay no regard (Shav - regard) to lying words (Sheqer - falsehoods)."

9. So the taskmasters (Sareh - officers) and the foremen (Shoter - overseers) of the people went out and said to the people, "Thus says Pharaoh, 'I will not give you straw.

10. Go and get your straw yourselves wherever you can find it, but your work will not be reduced (Sha'ar - diminished) in the least.'"

11. So the people were scattered (Pazar - scattered) throughout all the land of Egypt to gather stubble for straw.

12. The taskmasters (Sareh - officers) were urgent, saying, "Complete (Shalam - complete) your work, your daily task, each day, as when there was straw."

13. And the foremen (Shoter - overseers) of the people were beaten and were asked, "Why have you not done your task in making bricks as in the past (Re'shon - former days)?"

14. Then the foremen (Shoter - overseers) of the people came and cried out to Pharaoh, "Why do you treat your servants like this?

15. No straw is given to your servants, yet they say to us, 'Make bricks!' And behold, your servants are beaten, but the fault (Avon - guilt) is in your own people."

16. But he said, "You are idle, lazy! That is why you say, 'Let us go and sacrifice (Zabach - offer) to the Lord (YHWH - Lord).'

17. Go now and work (Avad - work), for no straw will be given to you, but you must still deliver (Natan - deliver) the same number of bricks."

18. The foremen (Shoter - overseers) of the people went out and they met Moses (Mosheh - drawn out) and Aaron (Aharon - exalted), who were waiting (Chakah - waiting) for them, as they came out from Pharaoh;

19. And they said to them, "The Lord (YHWH - Lord) look upon you and judge, because you have made us stink (Qe'ah - stink) in the sight (Ayne - eyes) of Pharaoh and his servants, and have put (Natan - put) a sword (Cherev - sword) in their hand to kill us."

20. Then Moses (Mosheh - drawn out) turned (Shuv - turned) to the Lord (YHWH - Lord) and said, "O Lord (Adonai - Lord), why have you done evil to this people? Why did you ever send me?

21. For since I came to Pharaoh to speak in your name, he has done evil to this people, and you have not delivered (Natsal - saved) your people at all."
