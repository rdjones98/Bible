1. And the Lord (YHWH - Lord) said to Moses (Mosheh - drawn out), "See, I have made you like God (Elohim - God) to Pharaoh, and your brother Aaron (Aharon - exalted) shall be your prophet (Navi - prophet).

2. You shall speak all that I command you, and your brother Aaron (Aharon - exalted) shall tell Pharaoh to let the people of Israel (Yisrael - God contends) go (Shalach - send) out of his land.

5. The Egyptians shall know that I am the Lord (YHWH - Lord), when I stretch out my hand over Egypt and bring (Yats'a - bring out) the people of Israel (Yisrael - God contends) from among them."

6. Moses (Mosheh - drawn out) and Aaron (Aharon - exalted) did just as the Lord (YHWH - Lord) commanded them.

7. Now Moses (Mosheh - drawn out) was 80 years old, and Aaron (Aharon - exalted) was 83 years old when they spoke (Dabar - spoke) to Pharaoh.

10. So Moses (Mosheh - drawn out) and Aaron (Aharon - exalted) went to Pharaoh and did just as the Lord (YHWH - Lord) commanded. Aaron (Aharon - exalted) cast down his staff before Pharaoh and his servants (Avad - servants), and it became (Haya - became) a serpent (Tanin - serpent).

11. Then Pharaoh summoned the wise men and the sorcerers, and they, the magicians of Egypt, also did the same by their secret arts (Kesem - magic).

12. For each man (Ish - man) cast down his staff, and they became (Haya - became) serpents. But Aaron's (Aharon - exalted) staff swallowed up their staffs (Mateh - staffs).

13. Still Pharaoh's heart was hardened, and he would not listen to them, as the Lord (YHWH - Lord) had said.

14. Then the Lord (YHWH - Lord) said to Moses (Mosheh - drawn out), "Pharaoh's heart is hardened; he refuses to let the people go (Shalach - send).

15. Go (Bo - go) to Pharaoh in the morning; as he is going out to the water, stand on the bank of the Nile, and take in your hand the staff that turned into a serpent (Tanin - serpent).

17. Thus says (Amar - says) the Lord (YHWH - Lord), "By this you shall know that I am the Lord (YHWH - Lord): behold, with the staff that is in my hand I will strike the water that is in the Nile, and it shall turn into blood.

18. The fish in the Nile shall die (Muwt - die), and the Nile will stink, and the Egyptians will be unable (Yachal - unable) to drink water from the Nile.'"

And there shall be blood throughout all the land of Egypt, even in vessels (Keli - vessels) of wood (Ets - wood) and in vessels (Keli - vessels) of stone (Even - stone).'"
