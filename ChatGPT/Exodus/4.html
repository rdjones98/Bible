Exodus 4

1. Then Moses (Mosheh - drawn out) answered (Anah - answered), "But behold, they will not believe (Aman - believe) me or listen to my voice (Kol - voice), for they will say, 'The Lord (YHWH - Lord) did not appear (Ra'ah - appear) to you.'"

2. The Lord (YHWH - Lord) said to him, "What is that in your hand?" He said, "A staff (Matteh - staff)."

3. And He said, "Throw it on the ground (Erets - ground)." So he threw it on the ground (Erets - ground), and it became a serpent (Tanin - serpent), and Moses (Mosheh - drawn out) ran (Nadad - ran) from it.

4. But the Lord (YHWH - Lord) said to Moses (Mosheh - drawn out), "Put out your hand and catch (Chazak - take hold of) it by the tail (Zon - tail)." So he put out his hand and caught (Chazak - took hold of) it, and it became a staff (Matteh - staff) in his hand.

5. "That they may believe (Aman - believe) that the Lord (YHWH - Lord) the God (Elohim - God) of their fathers (Avot - fathers), the God (Elohim - God) of Abraham (Avraham - father of many), the God (Elohim - God) of Isaac (Yitzhak - laughter), and the God (Elohim - God) of Jacob (Yaakov - supplanted), has appeared (Ra'ah - appeared) to you."

6. Again, the Lord (YHWH - Lord) said to him, "Put your hand inside your cloak (Chalokeh - cloak)." And he put his hand inside his cloak (Chalokeh - cloak), and when he took it out (Yatsa - took it out), behold, his hand was leprous (Tzara'at - leprous), like snow (Sheleg - snow).

7. Then God (Elohim - God) said, "Put your hand back inside your cloak (Chalokeh - cloak)." So he put his hand back inside his cloak (Chalokeh - cloak), and when he took it out (Yatsa - took it out), behold, it was restored (Shav - restored) like the rest of his flesh (Basar - flesh).

8. "If they will not believe (Aman - believe) you or listen to the first sign (Oth - sign), they may believe (Aman - believe) the latter sign (Oth - sign).

9. If they will not believe (Aman - believe) even these two signs or listen to your voice (Kol - voice), you shall take some water from the Nile (Yor - river) and pour (Shafak - pour) it on the dry ground (Yabashah - dry ground), and the water that you take from the Nile (Yor - river) will become blood (Damm - blood) on the dry ground (Yabashah - dry ground)."

10. But Moses (Mosheh - drawn out) said to the Lord (YHWH - Lord), "O my Lord (Adonai - Lord), I am not eloquent (Ish - man), either in the past (Bikdeh - before) or since you have spoken (Diber - spoken) to your servant (Eved - servant), but I am slow of speech (Kaved - heavy) and of tongue (Lashon - tongue)."

11. The Lord (YHWH - Lord) said to him, "Who has made (Asah - made) man's (Adam - man) mouth (Peh - mouth)? Who makes (Asah - makes) him mute (Cheresh - mute) or deaf (Kheresh - deaf), or seeing (Ra'ah - seeing) or blind (Iver - blind)? Is it not I, the Lord (YHWH - Lord)?

12. Now therefore go (Lech - go), and I will be with your mouth (Peh - mouth) and teach (Yarah - teach) you what you shall speak (Daber - speak)."

13. But he said, "O my Lord (Adonai - Lord), please send (Shalach - send) someone else (Acher - other)."

14. Then the anger (Af - anger) of the Lord (YHWH - Lord) was kindled (Charah - kindled) against Moses (Mosheh - drawn out) and he said, "Is there not Aaron (Aharon - exalted) your brother, the Levite (Levi - joined)? I know that he can speak (Daber - speak) well (Tov - well). Behold, he is coming out (Yatsa - coming) to meet (Qara - meet) you, and when he sees (Ra'ah - sees) you, he will be glad (Sameach - glad) in his heart.

15. You shall speak (Daber - speak) to him and put the words (Dabar - words) in his mouth (Pe - mouth), and I will be with your mouth (Pe - mouth) and with his mouth (Pe - mouth) and will teach (Yarah - teach) you both what to do.

16. He shall speak (Daber - speak) for you to the people, and he shall be your mouth (Pe - mouth), and you shall be as God (Elohim - God) to him.

17. And take (Lakach - take) in your hand this staff (Matteh - staff) with which you shall do the signs."

18. Moses (Mosheh - drawn out) went back (Shuv - went back) to Jethro (Yitro - His excellency), his father-in-law (Choten - father-in-law), and said to him, "Please let me go back (Shuv - return) to my brothers (Ach - brothers) in Egypt (Mitzrayim - Egypt), to see whether they are still alive (Chayah - alive)." And Jethro (Yitro - His excellency) said to Moses (Mosheh - drawn out), "Go (Lech - go) in peace (Shalom - peace)."

19. And the Lord (YHWH - Lord) said to Moses (Mosheh - drawn out) in Midian (Midyan - strife), "Go (Lech - go) back (Shuv - return) to Egypt (Mitzrayim - Egypt), for all the men (Anashim - men) who were seeking your life (Chayah - life) are dead (Muth - dead)."

20. So Moses (Mosheh - drawn out) took (Lakach - took) his wife (Ishto - wife) and his sons (Banim - sons) and had them ride (Rakav - ride) on a donkey (Chamor - donkey), and went back (Shuv - returned) to the land of Egypt (Mitzrayim - Egypt). And Moses (Mosheh - drawn out) took (Lakach - took) the staff (Matteh - staff) of God (Elohim - God) in his hand.

21. And the Lord (YHWH - Lord) said to Moses (Mosheh - drawn out), "When you go back (Shuv - return) to Egypt (Mitzrayim - Egypt), see that you do before Pharaoh all the miracles that I have put (Natan - given) in your power, but I will harden (Chazaq - harden) his heart, so that he will not let the people go (Shalach - send).
