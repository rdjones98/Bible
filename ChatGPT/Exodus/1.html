Exodus 1

1. These are the names of the sons of Israel who came to Egypt with Jacob (Yaakov - supplanted), each with his household:

2. Reuben (Re'uven - behold, a son), Simeon (Shimon - heard), Levi (Levi - joined), Judah (Yehudah - praised),

3. Issachar (Yissachar - there is reward), Zebulun (Zevulun - dwelling), and Benjamin (Binyamin - son of the right hand),

4. Dan (Dan - judged), Naphtali (Naftali - my struggle), Gad (Gad - fortune), Asher (Asher - happy),

5. Issachar (Yissachar - there is reward), Zebulun (Zevulun - dwelling), and Benjamin (Binyamin - son of the right hand),

6. Joseph (Yosef - he will add) and his brothers all died,

7. But the Israelites (Bnei - sons of Israel) were fruitful and increased greatly and became exceedingly strong, so that the land was filled with them.

8. Now there arose a new king over Egypt, who did not know Joseph (Yosef - he will add).

9. And he said to his people, "Behold, the people of the children (Bnei - children) of Israel are too many and too mighty for us.

10. Come, let us deal shrewdly (Hithal - deal wisely) with them, lest they multiply, and, if war breaks out, they join our enemies and fight against us and escape (Natsal - escape) from the land."

11. Therefore they set (Sam - set) taskmasters over them to afflict them with heavy burdens. They built for Pharaoh store cities, Pithom (Pitom - ruins) and Ramses (Ra'amses - sun's son).

12. But the more they were oppressed, the more they multiplied and the more they spread abroad. And the Egyptians were in dread of the people of Israel.

13. So they ruthlessly made the people of Israel work as slaves and made their lives bitter (Mar - bitter) with hard service in mortar (Chemer - mortar) and brick (Leven - brick) and in all manner of work in the field (Sadeh - field); all their work they made them do with rigor (Chomah - severity).

14. Then the king of Egypt spoke to the Hebrew midwives, one of whom was named Shiphrah (Shifrah - fair) and the other Puah (Pua - splendor).

15. When you help the Hebrew women give birth, and see them on the birthstool, if it is a son (Ben - son), then you shall kill him, but if it is a daughter (Bat - daughter), then she shall live (Chayah - live)."

16. But the midwives feared God (Elohim - Mighty Ones) and did not do as the king of Egypt commanded them, but let the male children (Ben - children) live.

17. So the king of Egypt the midwives and said to them, "Why have you done this and let the male children (Ben - children) live?"

18. The midwives said to Pharaoh, "Because the Hebrew women are not like the Egyptian women; for they are vigorous and give birth before the midwife (Meyaledet - midwife) comes to them."

19. So God dealt well with the midwives, and the people multiplied and grew very strong.

20. And because the midwives feared God, he gave them families.

21. Then Pharaoh commanded all his people, saying, "Every son that is born to the Hebrews you shall cast into the Nile, but every daughter you shall let live (Chayah - live)."
