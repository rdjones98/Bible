6. So he made ready (Chazaq - made ready) his chariot (Rekhev - chariot) and took (Lakah - took) his army (Tzavah - army) with him.

7. And he took six hundred (Shesh Me'ot - six hundred) chosen (Bachar - chosen) chariots (Rekhev - chariots), and all the other chariots (Rekhev - chariots) of Egypt (Mitzrayim - Egypt), with officers (Sar - officers) over all of them.

8. 9. The Egyptians (Mitzrayim - Egyptians) pursued (Radaph - pursued) them, all the horses (Sus - horses) and chariots (Rekhev - chariots) of Pharaoh (Par'oh - Pharaoh), and his horsemen (Parash - horsemen) and his army (Tzavah - army), and overtook (Asaf - overtook) them encamped (Chanah - encamped) at the sea (Yam - sea) by Pi-hahiroth (Pi Hachiroth - Pi-hahiroth), in front of Baal-zephon (Baal-Tsefon - Baal-zephon).

10. When Pharaoh (Par'oh - Pharaoh) drew near (Nagash - drew near), the people (Am - people) of Israel (Yisra'el - Israel) lifted up (Nasa - lifted up) their eyes (Ein - eyes), and behold (Hineh - behold), the Egyptians (Mitzrayim - Egyptians) were marching after them (Radaph - marching after them), and they were very afraid (Yare - afraid). And the people (Am - people) of Israel (Yisra'el - Israel) cried out (Zaqah - cried out) to the Lord (YHWH - He is).

11. They said (Amar - said) to Moses (Mosheh - drawn out), "Is it because there are no graves (Qever - graves) in Egypt (Mitzrayim - Egypt) that you have taken us away (Lakach - taken us away) to die (Muth - die) in the wilderness (Midbar - wilderness)? What have you done (Asah - done) to us in bringing us out (Yatsa - bringing us out) of Egypt (Mitzrayim - Egypt)?

12. Is not this what we said (Amar - said) to you in Egypt (Mitzrayim - Egypt), 'Leave us alone (Azav - leave us alone) that we may serve (Avad - serve) the Egyptians (Mitzrayim - Egyptians)'? For it would have been better (Tob - better) for us to serve (Avad - serve) the Egyptians (Mitzrayim - Egyptians) than to die (Muth - die) in the wilderness (Midbar - wilderness)."

13. And Moses (Mosheh - drawn out) said (Amar - said) to the people (Am - people), "Fear (Yare - fear) not, stand firm (Yatsab - stand firm), and see (Ra'ah - see) the salvation (Yeshua - salvation) of the Lord (YHWH - He is), which he will work (Asah - work) for you today (Yom - day). For the Egyptians (Mitzrayim - Egyptians) whom you see (Ra'ah - see) today (Yom - day), you shall never (Od - never) see again (Ra'ah - see) forever (Olam - forever).

14. The Lord (YHWH - He is) will fight (Lacham - fight) for you, and you have only (Rak - only) to be silent (Chashah - silent)."

15. The Lord (YHWH - He is) said (Amar - said) to Moses (Mosheh - drawn out), "Why do you cry (Tza'aq - cry) to me? Tell (Dabar - tell) the people (Am - people) of Israel (Yisra'el - Israel) to go forward (Nasa - go forward).

16. Lift up (Nasa - lift up) your staff (Mateh - staff), and stretch out (Natah - stretch out) your hand (Yad - hand) over the sea (Yam - sea), and divide (Baqa - divide) it, that the people (Am - people) of Israel (Yisra'el - Israel) may go through (Avar - go through) the sea (Yam - sea) on dry ground (Yabashah - dry ground).

17. And I will harden (Chazaq - harden) the hearts (Lev - hearts) of the Egyptians (Mitzrayim - Egyptians), so that they shall go in (Bo - go in) after them, and I will get glory (Kavod - glory) over Pharaoh (Par'oh - Pharaoh) and all his army (Tzavah - army), his chariots (Rekhev - chariots), and his horsemen (Parash - horsemen).

18. And the Egyptians (Mitzrayim - Egyptians) shall know (Yada - know) that I am the Lord (YHWH - He is), when I have gotten glory (Kavod - glory) over Pharaoh (Par'oh - Pharaoh), his chariots (Rekhev - chariots), and his horsemen (Parash - horsemen)."

19. Then the angel (Malak - angel) of God (Elohim - God) who was going (Halak - going) before the host (Machaneh - host) of Israel (Yisra'el - Israel) moved and went behind them, and the pillar (Amud - pillar) of cloud (Anan - cloud) moved from before them and stood (Amad - stood) behind them,

20. coming between (Badal - between) the camp (Machaneh - camp) of the Egyptians (Mitzrayim - Egyptians) and the camp (Machaneh - camp) of Israel (Yisra'el - Israel). And there was the cloud (Anan - cloud) and the darkness (Choshekh - darkness), and it lit up (Awr - lit up) the night (Lailah - night) without one coming near (Nagash - near) the other all night (Lailah - night).

21. Then Moses (Mosheh - drawn out) stretched out (Natah - stretched out) his hand (Yad - hand) over the sea (Yam - sea), and the Lord (YHWH - He is) drove the sea (Yam - sea) back by a strong (Chazaq - strong) east wind (Ruach - wind) all night (Lailah - night) and made the sea (Yam - sea) dry land (Yabashah - dry land), and the waters (Mayim - waters) were divided (Baqa - divided).

22. And the people (Am - people) of Israel (Yisra'el - Israel) went into the midst (Tavek - midst) of the sea (Yam - sea) on dry ground (Yabashah - dry ground), the waters (Mayim - waters) being a wall (Chomah - wall) to them on their right hand (Yad - hand) and on their left hand (Yad - hand).

23. The Egyptians (Mitzrayim - Egyptians) pursued (Radaph - pursued) and went in (Bo - went in) after them into the midst (Tavek - midst) of the sea (Yam - sea), all Pharaoh's (Par'oh - Pharaoh's) horses (Sus - horses), his chariots (Rekhev - chariots), and his horsemen (Parash - horsemen).

24. And in the morning watch (Boker - watch), the Lord (YHWH - He is) looked down (Nabat - looked down) on the Egyptian army (Tzavah - army) through the pillar (Amud - pillar) of fire (Esh - fire) and cloud (Anan - cloud), and threw (Hurl - threw) the Egyptian army (Tzavah - army) into a panic (Balah - panic).

25. 26. Then the Lord (YHWH - He is) said (Amar - said) to Moses (Mosheh - drawn out), "Stretch out (Natah - stretch out) your hand (Yad - hand) over the sea (Yam - sea), that the waters (Mayim - waters) may come back (Shub - come back) upon the Egyptians (Mitzrayim - Egyptians), upon their chariots (Rekhev - chariots), and upon their horsemen (Parash - horsemen)."

27. So Moses (Mosheh - drawn out) stretched out (Natah - stretched out) his hand (Yad - hand) over the sea (Yam - sea), and the sea (Yam - sea) returned (Shub - returned) to its normal course (Tav - course) when the morning appeared (Boker - appeared). And as the Egyptians (Mitzrayim - Egyptians) fled into it (Boa - fled), the Lord (YHWH - He is) threw (Hurl - threw) the Egyptians (Mitzrayim - Egyptians) into the midst (Tavek - midst) of the sea (Yam - sea).

Of all the host (Machaneh - host) of Pharaoh (Par'oh - Pharaoh) that had followed them into the sea (Yam - sea), not one of them remained (Sha'ar - remained).

29. But the people (Am - people) of Israel (Yisra'el - Israel) walked on dry ground (Yabashah - dry ground) through the sea (Yam - sea), the waters (Mayim - waters) being a wall (Chomah - wall) to them on their right hand (Yad - hand) and on their left hand (Yad - hand).

30. Thus the Lord (YHWH - He is) saved (Yasha - saved) Israel (Yisra'el - Israel) that day (Yom - day) from the hand (Yad - hand) of the Egyptians (Mitzrayim - Egyptians), and Israel (Yisra'el - Israel) saw (Ra'ah - saw) the Egyptians (Mitzrayim - Egyptians) dead (Muth - dead) on the seashore (Yam - shore).

31. Israel (Yisra'el - Israel) saw (Ra'ah - saw) the great (Gadol - great) power (Yad - power) that the Lord (YHWH - He is) used (Asah - used) against the Egyptians (Mitzrayim - Egyptians), so the people (Am - people) feared (Yare - feared) the Lord (YHWH - He is), and they believed (Aman - believed) in the Lord (YHWH - He is) and in his servant (Eved - servant) Moses (Mosheh - drawn out).
