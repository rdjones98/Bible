Exodus 11 - Straightforward Translation

1. The Lord (YHWH - Lord) said to Moses (Mosheh - drawn out), "Yet one plague (Negah - plague) more I will bring (Bo - bring) upon Pharaoh (Par'oh - Pharaoh) and upon Egypt (Mitzrayim - Egypt); afterward (Achar - afterward) he will let you go (Shalach - send) from here (Makam - place). When he lets you go (Shalach - send), he will drive you out (Garah - drive out) completely (Me'od - very).

2. Speak (Amar - speak) now (Na - now) in the hearing (Oz - hearing) of the people, that each man (Ish - man) ask (Sha'al - ask) of his neighbor (Re'a - neighbor) and each woman (Isha - woman) of her neighbor (Re'a - neighbor) for silver (Kesef - silver) and gold (Zahav - gold) jewelry (Tajm - jewelry).

3. And the Lord (YHWH - Lord) gave (Natan - gave) the people favor (Chen - favor) in the sight (Ein - eyes) of the Egyptians (Mitzrayim - Egypt). Moreover, the man (Ish - man) Moses (Mosheh - drawn out) was very great (Gadol - great) in the land (Eretz - land) of Egypt (Mitzrayim - Egypt), in the sight (Ein - eyes) of Pharaoh's (Par'oh - Pharaoh) servants, and in the sight (Ein - eyes) of the people.

4. So Moses (Mosheh - drawn out) said, "Thus says (Amar - says) the Lord (YHWH - Lord), 'About midnight (Chatzi - midnight) I will go out (Yatsa - go out) in the midst (Qerev - midst) of Egypt (Mitzrayim - Egypt),

5. and every firstborn (Bekhor - firstborn) in the land (Eretz - land) of Egypt (Mitzrayim - Egypt) shall die (Muwt - die), from the firstborn (Bekhor - firstborn) of Pharaoh (Par'oh - Pharaoh) who sits (Yashav - sits) on his throne (Kise - throne), even to the firstborn (Bekhor - firstborn) of the slave (Shifchah - slave) girl (Amah - maid) who is behind (Achar - behind) the millstones (Reheh - millstones), and all the firstborn (Bekhor - firstborn) of the cattle (Behemah - cattle).


Isis - Goddess of Motherhood (wearing a 'throne' crown).Osiris - God of the underworld, fertility and resurrection (mummified man). None of their gods or even Pharoah himself can protect their children or bring them back.

6. There shall be a great cry (Zaqah - cry) throughout all the land (Eretz - land) of Egypt (Mitzrayim - Egypt), such as there has been none like it, nor shall be like it again (Asah - again).

7. But against any of the children (Banim - children) of Israel (Yisra'el - Israel) a dog (Kelev - dog) shall not growl (Na'ah - growl), against man (Ish - man) or beast (Behemah - beast), that you may know (Yadah - know) that the Lord (YHWH - Lord) makes a distinction (Pala - distinguishes) between the Egyptians (Mitzrayim - Egypt) and Israel (Yisra'el - Israel).

8. And all these your servants shall come down (Yarad - come down) to me and bow down (Shachah - bow) to me, saying (Amar - saying), 'Get out (Yatsa - go out), you and all the people who follow (Achar - follow) you!' And after that (Achar - after that) I will go out (Yatsa - go out)." And he went out from Pharaoh (Par'oh - Pharaoh) in hot anger (Chomah - anger).

9. Then the Lord (YHWH - Lord) said to Moses (Mosheh - drawn out), "Pharaoh (Par'oh - Pharaoh) will not listen (Shama - listen) to you, that my wonders (Mopheth - wonders) may be multiplied (Rabah - multiplied) in the land (Eretz - land) of Egypt (Mitzrayim - Egypt)."

10. Moses (Mosheh - drawn out) and Aaron (Aharon - exalted) did all these wonders (Mopheth - wonders) before Pharaoh (Par'oh - Pharaoh), and the Lord (YHWH - Lord) hardened (Chazak - hardened) Pharaoh's (Par'oh - Pharaoh) heart (Lev - heart), and he did not let the children (Banim - children) of Israel (Yisra'el - Israel) go out (Yatsa - go out) from his land (Eretz - land).
